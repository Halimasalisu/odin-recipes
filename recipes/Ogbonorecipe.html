a brief description of how to prepare ogbono

Ogbono soup is a unique cultural delicacy from Nigeria. Although it is common amongst houselholds accross country, ogbono originated in the southwestern part of Nigeria amongst the Igbos, Yoruba, Ijaw, Calabar etc. This soup is made with all manner of assoreted ingredients like fish, meat, pkomo (cow skin), crayfish etc.

It is prepared by heating water to a certain temperature (please don't ask me what this temperature is. As a proper Nigerian, if you have cooked this food that many times you will just no the temperature by instinct). The main ingredient the dried, grounded, seeds pf the African mango or as our caucasian counterparts with the inate desire of making life a little bit complex for the rest of us (also called scientists) named it Irvingia So after heating the water to a minimum temperature you can start adding your ingredients.

The following is an ordered list of the ingredients needed to prepare ogbono soup

  1. boiling water
  2. African bush mango seeds (the dried ones)
  3. Palm oil
  4. Maggi or any seasoning of your choice
  5. Salt
  6. Crayfish
  7. Meat or fish or you can use both
    8. important note:
    once you have every ingredient listed above you should put them in one after the other in order or not inorder. Anyhow you feel like it just taste and wait for it to properly cook.
      Pour in cups of water into the pot Allow the water to heat to a certain temperature When the water is hot enough (use your intuition to figure that out on your own) Put the granded seeds of Ogbono inside a plate and on top of the hot water While the water is boiling, prepare the rest of the ingredients and wait Then put each ingredients inside (you can do it all at once, you can do it one at a time anyone) Then wait till the Ogbono soup cooks properly.
